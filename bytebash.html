What is Byte Bash?

Byte Bashes are free events hosted 2-3 times a year by the MHS Byte Club to promote computer science, programming, coding, and development. Each event is a combination of fun tutorials, contests, and challenges designed to increase understanding and interest in coding and computer science. This one is version 3.1 because it's the first bash of the 3rd year of Byte Club!

Byte Bash is a non-competitive event and no prior experience is necessary. The event is open to all current or future MHS students (and faculty)! Your are welcome to attend the tutorials and events even if you cannot stay for the entire time. Though we ask that you give it a solid shot by staying at least 90 minutes. If you're brand new to coding, it takes a while to get up and running!

Event Details

  • Date: Wednesday, November 16th, 2016 from 3:30 PM until 7:00 PM
  • Location: Mundelein High School Coding Lab (Room C104)
  • Who's Invited: Any MHS student, faculty, staff member, or sender school students

What if I don't know how to code?
Don't worry! We're all in some stage of the learning process. Though the first step can seem like the toughest, we want you to take it with us!

What if I don't have a computer?
No problem. We've got 2 state-of-the-art labs full of desktops waiting for you to use. We've also got Chromebooks and Android tablets to check out for the event.

What if I can't stay the whole time?
Coding is fun, but it can take a little while to get up and running. We ask that you give it a shot with us for at least 90 minutes. We promise you'll leave having learned something cool and/or new!

I'm a student at an area sender school, can I attend the event!?
Absolutely! The MHS Byte club would love to hear from you. One of our primary goals is to spread computer science and coding to the entire Mundelein community. Contact us for more information.

Is guest wi-fi available if I want to bring my own laptop?
Yes! Guest wi-fi is available and you are encouraged to bring your own machine to use for the event, complete with any IDEs or other programs you like to use. Please note: Any machine using the guest wi-fi is subject to the D120 Acceptable Use Policy.

Instructional Sessions

Create an 'Impossible Game' Screen

Contribute to the Byte Me game by learning JavaScript and Processing.js. Create a graphical game screen that requires users to perform some simple task to get to the next screen. Work with mentors to make your ideas come to life.

Skill level: Beginner

Introductory Web Development

A little goes a long way with this introductory session on HTML/CSS/JS. The three languages of the web work together to create dynamic web pages. Go beyond the limits of Google Sites or other page builders and code a beautiful webpage for yourself!

Skill level: Beginner/Intermediate

Android Apps with App Inventor

Create your first mobile app using free cloud-based development tools like MIT App Inventor. Leave with a basic app that can be downloaded and installed on any Android device!

Skill level: Beginner/Intermediate

Basic CS for Teachers!

Do you teach a subject that includes logic? Do you address content that includes mathematics? Do your courses include the use of a language (you know, like, words)? Then you can use computer science to address important concepts! Stop by and learn the basics of computer logic and how CS can help your students can become teachers!

Skill Level: Faculty!

Contests and Challenges

Character Contest

Use Khan Academy's ProcessingJS module to create the most creative, entertaining, or complex character you can. Learn the basics of animating/drawing with Javascript. Check out last year's winner: Ninja Frog by Victoria Ozymko. Anyone can enter, anyone can win!

See full rules.

Webpage Contest

Create the best-looking webpage you can with just a little bit of training in HTML, CSS, and Javascript. Build a responsive interactive webpage and learn to make your site intuitive and easy-to-use. Best looking webpage wins!

See full rules.

Mathing Contest

Out-math your fellow programmers with this logical and mathematical competition. Work with a partner to try and solve a series of mathematical or logical problems using the programming language of your choice!

See full rules.

The Byte Club 5

Brand new to coding? Try the Byte Club 5. A series of small coding challenges to get the feel for any new computer language. You can use any of the resources listed below to help you with this task. Get started on your CS journey now!

See full rules.

First, thanks for noticing. Second, as part of Byte Club we try to work as closely to the actual code as we can.
