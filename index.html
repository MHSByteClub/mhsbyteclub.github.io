Come Learn with Us!

3 for Free begins next Tuesday, April 12th

The MHS Byte Club wants to unlock your inner programmer. Sign up for any of 3 free instructional sessions during Tuesdays in April. Use this form to sign up for any or all of the sessions below! Get smarter and code with us.

All sessions take place in room A102 beginning at 3:30 PM

  • Tuesday, 4/12 - Basic Jeopardy

    • Instructor: Rygar Carvajal, Byte Club Vice President
    • Description: Build your very own trivia game using your very own questions. Program the game to keep score and pick a winner.
    • Bring your own flash drive and leave with a basic, custom Jeopardy game you can add to!

  • Tuesday, 4/19 - Web Design and Development

    • Instructor: Michael Voell, Guest Presenter
    • Description: Learn to create professional-looking pages and learn to develop interactive web elements with a design/development oriented workshop!

  • Tuesday, 4/26 - Adventures with C++

    • Instructor: Kenny Stepney, Byte Club President
    • Description: Create your very own maze game with the mother of all languages: C++. Together, we will learn the basics on how to create a application in C++, as well as understand the structure behind applications. Even if you’re not interested in creating a maze game, don’t worry, it’s still a great opportunity to learn more about applications and C++. You will definitely find this project well worth your time here at Byte Club.
    • Bring your own flash drive and leave with a simple customized map game!

  • Tuesday, 5/3 - The Final CodeDown

    • Coding Skills Competition/Pro-Am
    • New and experienced programmers team up to compete in a series of fun coding challenges for glorious (edible) prizes! Whether you're super hacker or a new script kiddie,get it on the fun!

  • More About 3for Free

    • No Experience Necessary!
    • Just sign-up to let us know you're coming. Bring a friend
    • The sessions run between 1-2 hours, but you can still participate even if you have to leave early.
    • You can participate in as many sessions as you like, but please only sign up if you intend to be there!

Hello, World!

This is the homepage for the Mundelein High School programming club. We provide an environment for MHS students to practice and learn to program. We're newly founded so there'll be more to see here later. (^̮^)

Goal for this Club

  • Give people a chance to learn and program with others

Projects we are currently doing or will be doing

Some things we'll definitely be working on:

  • Chrome extensions: MHS issues chromebooks to students, which means our extensions can have a big impact. For example, the school CIPA filter blocks a lot of relevant sites, but visiting a site to find that it's blocked is annoying and time-wasting. We made a chrome extension that lets you know whether a link is blocked or not, and now our lives are bliss.
  • Processing sketches: For most new members, byte club will be your first time coding, and processing/processing.js is an ideal way to begin as it allows you to write visual programs easily and immediately. You can make cool art, interactive demonstrations, and much more!
    • There's also a great processing curriculum and editor at Khanacademy.org
  • Hackathons: These are events where programmers get together and build cool things in a short span of time. Some (unaffiliated) examples of hackathons you could attend are CodeDay or any listed at MLH.
  • Website building: Even if you don't want to make websites, it's cool to understand how they work. Learning HTML/CSS is a great way to start! Khanacademy.org and Codecademy.org are good beginner tools.

Things we have worked on:

  • Impossible Game Challenge: We made a fun club project to put our Khanacademy programming skills to use. The club members made mini-challenges that were put together to make one big impossible game.
  • Small personal sites: Enjoy our member websites in miniature form!
  • Graduation seating arranger: Mrs. Billitier asked us to help her out when organizing which graduate sits where for graduation. This program is the start of our help.
  • Mustang Match Maker: This suurvey of love is Byte Club's most famous creation! We hope you enjoy our prediction of your best Mustang Match.
  • March Madness: We had an idea to make a programming leaderboard game similar to March Madness, but this is as far as we got.

    • Codecademy Offers great tutorials on Web development (HTML/CSS/JavaScript/JQuery/Ruby/PHP) and Python.
    • KhanAcademy Great editor for visual javascript programs. Also includes a full suite of videos to teach you how to program.
    • Bento Includes lots of great links to programming tutorials.
    • CS50 Harvard intro programming course. Teaches you computer science, languages used include include C, PHP, and JavaScript plus SQL, CSS, and HTML
    • Udacity Offers a wide variety of Computer Science courses from beginner intro courses to advanced "holy cow we're making a self-driving car" courses. Includes really great introductory programming course on Python, although all of its courses are great.
